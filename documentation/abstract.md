# Abstract

Understanding whether a protein positively or negatively regulates disease is essential for therapeutic target identification and precision medicine.

This project presents an evidence-centric artificial intelligence (AI) framework that integrates biomedical literature retrieval, BioBERT-assisted sentence extraction, and rule-based experimental filtering to infer protein–disease regulatory relationships.

Unlike conventional predictive approaches, the framework relies exclusively on experimentally validated evidence to ensure transparency, interpretability, and traceability.

Regulatory statements are classified into positive, negative, or neutral categories before being filtered according to experimental context, including knockout studies, clinical investigations, and functional assays.

A weighted evidence aggregation strategy combines regulatory information across multiple studies to infer consensus regulatory roles while preserving links to supporting PubMed records.

The framework provides a scalable, reproducible, and biologically interpretable approach for systematic literature mining in computational biology and drug discovery.
