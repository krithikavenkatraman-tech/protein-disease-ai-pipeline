# Research Gap

Biomedical literature contains extensive experimentally validated information describing protein regulation in disease.

Despite this abundance of knowledge, existing literature mining approaches primarily identify protein–disease associations without determining:

- Direction of regulation
- Experimental context
- Biological evidence strength

Many AI-driven systems rely on predictive or black-box models that provide limited interpretability and cannot easily trace predictions back to experimentally validated studies.

As biomedical publications continue to grow rapidly, there is an increasing need for computational frameworks capable of producing transparent, evidence-based regulatory inference while maintaining biological interpretability.

This project addresses these limitations through an evidence-centric computational workflow that prioritizes experimentally validated literature over purely predictive modeling.
